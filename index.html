Highlighted Projects

Constellation (2025)

Leveraging the MTMT publication and creative artwork database, we built an interactive visualization dashboard that reveals the sprawling collaboration network of MOME (Moholy-Nagy University of Art & Design).

To push the boundaries further, we crafted a 3D version—visually inspired by the galaxy map from Elite Dangerous—offering a cosmic perspective on academic and creative connections.

IMDb Competency Map (2025)

Ever wondered if a person's skills and expertise could be mapped into vector space to enable intelligent matchmaking for collaborations? We did too.

Our pilot explored that very idea using the IMDb dataset—mining movie and crew metadata to build a prototype of a "competency map." By experimenting with graph neural networks and fine-tuning SentenceTransformers models (MPNet), we turned raw textual role data into meaningful embeddings that reflect not just what someone did, but how they fit into a wider network of expertise.

Pilot

XX Trianguli: The most spotted star in the sky (2024)

Teamed up with astronomers from the Konkoly Observatory and the Leibniz Institute for Astrophysics Potsdam for a Nature Communications paper exploring stellar surface activity in the chaotic dynamo system of XX Trianguli. Our data science team contributed interpolation models and visual storytelling assets that helped visualize the star’s dynamic and spot-rich surface in unprecedented detail.

The article landed in the 99th percentile (top 1%) of all Altmetric-tracked papers and ranked #61 out of 2,438 Nature Communications articles of the same age. It was named one of Nature’s Top 25 Physics Papers of 2024 — sparking international press coverage, social media traction, and academic follow-ups.

Thanks to Zsolt Kővári and Viktor Varga for making this project possible and stellar!

Corpus analysis of the Buddhist monk Taixu (2024)

In collaboration with sinologist Peter Boros we applied natural language processing to examine Chinese philosophical discourse in the writings of Taixu (太虛大師; 1890–1947). I led the development of a custom text mining pipeline to uncover linguistic patterns and conceptual shifts across a carefully curated corpus of modern Buddhist literature.

ReConnect China: A co-publication and co-patenting analysis (2023)

As part of the Horizon Europe initiative, I collaborated with the Centre for Social Innovation to investigate the evolving landscape of EU–China research collaboration. My role focused on large-scale bibliometric and network analysis, mapping scientific co-authorship and patenting trends using the Web of Science and the 2023 PATSTAT release.
